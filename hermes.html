Hermés

Thierry Hermès was born in Krefeld, Germany, to a French father and a German mother. The family moved to France in 1828.[4] In 1837, Hermès first established a harness workshop in the Grands Boulevards quarter of Paris, dedicated to serving European noblemen.[5][6] He created high-quality wrought harnesses and bridles for the carriage trade,[7] winning several awards including the first prize in its class in 1855 and again in 1867 at the Expositions Universelles in Paris.[7][8] Hermès's son, Charles-Émile,[4] took over management from his father in 1880 and moved the shop to 24 rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré, where it remains. With the help of his sons Adolphe and Émile-Maurice, Charles-Émile introduced saddlery and started selling his products retail.[7] The company catered to the élite of Europe, North Africa, Russia, Asia, and the Americas. In 1900, the firm offered the Haut à Courroies bag, specially designed for riders to carry their saddles with them.
